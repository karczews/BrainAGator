Privacy Policy

Brainagator – Let's Learn & Play!

Application: Brainagator Package: io.github.karczews.brainagator Effective date: 1 January 2025 Last updated: March 2025

📋 Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the Brainagator application ("App", "we", "us") handles information when you or your child uses Brainagator on Android, iOS, Web, or Desktop.

We designed Brainagator with children's privacy as a core principle. The short version: we collect no personal data whatsoever.

Brainagator does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information about you or your child.

🔍 What Information We Collect

Brainagator collects no personal information. Specifically, the App does not collect or have access to:

  • Names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any contact details
  • Device identifiers, advertising IDs, or IP addresses
  • Location data (precise or approximate)
  • Microphone input or camera access
  • Photos, files, or any content stored on the device
  • Usage statistics or analytics
  • Crash reports sent to third-party services
  • Cookies or persistent tracking identifiers

The App requires no account registration and no login of any kind.

⚙️ How We Use Information

Because we collect no information, there is nothing to use, process, or store beyond what the operating system temporarily holds in RAM while the App is running. All game state (e.g., current puzzle, score) exists only in memory and is discarded when the App is closed.

There are no user profiles, no progress sync, and no data persisted to any server.

🔊 Text-to-Speech (Voice Guidance)

Brainagator uses the device's built-in text-to-speech (TTS) engine to read game instructions aloud. This processing happens entirely on-device:

  • Android: uses the Android TTS framework (on-device engine)
  • iOS: uses AVSpeechSynthesizer (on-device engine)
  • Desktop / Web: uses the Web Speech API (browser's local synthesis)

No audio is recorded, captured, or transmitted. The App only produces speech — it never listens through the microphone.

🤝 Information We Share

We do not share any information with third parties because we do not collect any. Brainagator contains:

  • No advertising SDKs
  • No analytics libraries (e.g., Firebase Analytics, Amplitude, Mixpanel)
  • No crash-reporting services that transmit data externally
  • No social-login integrations
  • No in-app purchase frameworks that share identity data

The only third-party library used for logging is Kermit, which writes diagnostic messages to the local device log only. These logs are never transmitted off the device.

👧 Children's Privacy (COPPA & GDPR-K)

Brainagator is designed for and intended to be used by children. We take children's privacy seriously and have designed the App to comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and equivalent regulations in other jurisdictions, including GDPR's provisions for children.

Because we collect absolutely no personal information, there is no data about children (or adults) to protect, delete, or transfer. No parental consent mechanism is required.

🗂️ Data Retention

No user data is stored by Brainagator. All runtime state is held in device memory and cleared when the App is closed. Uninstalling the App removes all App-related data from your device.

⚖️ Your Rights

Under regulations such as GDPR and CCPA, users have rights regarding their personal data (access, correction, deletion, portability, etc.). Since Brainagator holds no personal data about any user, these rights are satisfied by design — there is nothing to access, correct, or delete.

🔐 Security

Brainagator makes no network connections to external servers. All processing is entirely local to your device. This architecture eliminates network-based risks such as data interception or server breaches.

📝 Changes to This Policy

If the App's data practices ever change in a way that affects this policy, we will update this page and revise the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the App after any changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

We do not anticipate any changes that would involve the collection of personal data. Such a change would be clearly communicated and, where required by law, would require fresh consent.
